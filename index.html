Question Two

Please draft a reply to an email from a security researcher reporting that they have found an XSS in Basecamp, leading to full account takeover. How would you acknowledge the report, and what questions would you ask to help triage the issue? If you’d like to describe your thought process too, that would be really helpful.

This is honestly not something that I've had experience doing before. At my current employer, the teams that triage issues like this are separated from support/engineering, so this is something that I had to do some research on. I'm thankful for the opportunity to learn and stretch my wings, and I'm confident that this is a skill that I can develop further.

"Hi Danesh,

We appreciate your report. The security of our customer's data is our highest priority, so if you could provide me with a few more details we can begin this triage process immediately.

* Would you mind providing me with the steps that you took so that we can reproduce the issue?

* Would you mind providing any relevant screenshots of the process? The clearer picture we can provide the faster we can resolve this issue.

Thank you again for your time. As I wait for the requested details I will alert our internal teams to this potential vulnerability.

Regards,
Jess Pendley"

While waiting on the details for the follow up email I would alert relevant internal personel to the issue and check for any similar reports that are already being triaged.

Question Three

Please describe what steps you would take to diagnose a report that some images aren’t loading in a customer’s Basecamp account.

All of this is assuming that I am the person in control of communication with the customer. If not, then I would proceed through these steps in tandem with the assigned support rep and provide them with next steps for the customer.

1) I would ask the customer if these images were loading as expected previously, or if these are newly added images. If the images were just added, I would ask for the file types and sizes to make sure that they're supported/within parameters. I would also verify whether this is happening across all of their projects or if this is isolated to a single project.

"Hi Kim,

I am looking into this for you, but before we proceed I just need you to verify a few things for me.

Are these newly added images or did they exist in the project previously and now won't load? If they are new, would you mind providing me with copies of the images? Would you also verify the project that's experiencing this issue?

Regards,
Jess Pendley"

2) I would impersonate into the account in question and verify the behavior that the customer is experiencing.

3) I would email the customer, explaining that I can see the issue and am currently working toward resolution.

"Hi Kim,

I can see exactly what you're talking about. Let me dig into this further and I'll update as soon as I have more information.

Regards,
Jess Pendley"

4) I would check backend data for any tell-tale error messages and follow those accordingly. If the error message is one that is resolvable by myself and/or my team, I would proceed accordingly. If the error message pointed to a previously unknown issue or an issue outside of the scope of my team, I would take the necessary steps to engage dev or ops.

5) I would email the customer again, with either my solution or confirmation of next steps and next expected communication.

"Hi Kim,

After researching this further it looks like this may be something that our internal dev team will need to take a deeper look at. I will update you as soon as I have more information, or by tomorrow afternoon at the latest.

In the interim, let me know if you need anything else.

Regards,
Jess Pendley"
